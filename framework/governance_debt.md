# Governance Debt (GD)


**Governance Debt (GD)** is the accumulated operational risk created when autonomous system capability grows faster than governance infrastructure.


The concept is analogous to technical debt in software engineering.


When organizations deploy increasingly autonomous agent systems without corresponding accountability mechanisms, hidden governance liabilities accumulate.


Governance Debt compounds quietly.


The liability typically surfaces later:


- During regulatory audits  
- During enterprise security reviews  
- During customer disputes  
- During incident investigations  


The cost of governance debt is rarely immediate.


It is deferred.


Often with interest.


Over time, governance debt increases the operational fragility of autonomous systems and raises the cost of maintaining accountability as autonomy scales.
